Compound Interest Calculator

Compound interest is often called the eighth wonder of the world because it makes money grow exponentially. Unlike simple interest, compound interest adds earned interest back into the principal, allowing your money to grow faster.

The formula is:

A = P (1 + r/n) ^ nt

Where:

  • A = Total amount
  • P = Principal
  • r = Interest rate
  • n = Number of times interest is compounded per year
  • t = Time (in years)

In the 21K Tools calculator, you can:

  • Choose compounding frequency (Annually, Semi-Annually, Quarterly, Monthly, Weekly, or Daily)
  • Add regular contributions (like monthly SIPs or deposits)
